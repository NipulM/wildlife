Discover the Diverse Wildlife of Sri Lanka

Introduction

Welcome to the enchanting world of Sri Lanka's indigenous animals. This page offers a glimpse into the rich biodiversity that graces the island nation. From majestic elephants to elusive leopards, Sri Lanka is home to a fascinating array of wildlife that captivates nature enthusiasts and conservationists alike.

Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus inornatus)

Description

The sloth bear has a shaggy black coat and distinctive white V-shaped markings on its chest. It has long, curved claws adapted for climbing and feeding.

Behavior

Sloth bears are primarily nocturnal and have a unique habit of carrying cubs on their backs, providing protection.

Habitat

Found in various forested habitats, including dry and moist deciduous forests.

Purple-faced Langur (Semnopithecus vetulus)

Description

Also known as the purple-faced leaf monkey, this primate species has a dark face with a purple hue. It has a long tail and is adapted for an arboreal lifestyle.

Habitat

Endemic to Sri Lanka, these langurs inhabit both lowland and montane rainforests.

Conservation

Faces threats from habitat loss and fragmentation. Conservation efforts focus on preserving their forest habitats.

Spotted Deer (Axis axis ceylonensis)

Description

The Sri Lankan subspecies of spotted deer has a reddish-brown coat with white spots. Males have antlers.

Habitat

Found in a range of habitats, including grasslands, forests, and agricultural areas.

Conservation

While not currently endangered, habitat loss and hunting pose threats to their populations.
