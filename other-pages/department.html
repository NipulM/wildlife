About the Department of Wildlife Conservation

Since its inception in 1949, the Department of Wildlife Conservation has been an unwavering guardian of Sri Lanka's rich wildlife heritage. Originating from the late 19th century initiatives led by Colonel Clark, the department has evolved into a dedicated force, addressing challenges and preserving the island's unique fauna and flora. Today, amidst contemporary challenges, the department remains committed to sustainability, ensuring the enduring protection of Sri Lanka's natural treasures.

Empowering Conservation, Embracing Tomorrow

In 1993, the department took a pivotal step with the launch of the "Development of Wildlife Conservation and Protected Area Management" project. This initiative symbolizes the department's proactive vision, featuring an expanded team, heightened wildlife research efforts, and targeted strategies for the conservation of wild elephants. Positioned for a harmonious coexistence between wildlife and the evolving needs of humanity, the department exemplifies its dedication to a sustainable and thriving future.

Protected Areas

Sri Lanka's protected areas, managed by the Department of Wildlife Conservation and Forest Department, play a pivotal role in biodiversity conservation. Amid habitat loss from rapid human growth and development, these zones act as refuges, prohibiting human intervention to ensure species survival. Beyond protecting biodiversity, these areas safeguard ecosystems, cultural sites, and serve as crucial reference points for monitoring environmental changes due to human impact.
